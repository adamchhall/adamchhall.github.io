About Me

Hello! My name is Adam, a statistics PhD from the University of Michigan. My research advisors were Roderick Little, and Matthew Shapiro. My faculty mentor was Ben Hansen. I'm interested in methods for learning about and improving the social and political world we inhabit, and anything else that's cool.

In my spare time, I like to read about politics, history, and philosophy, play video games, tinker with computers, and go hiking.

I recieved my B.A. in Quantitative Economics and Sociology from the University of California, Irvine in 2011. I am a member of Golden Key and Phi Beta Kappa, and a recipient of the Rackham Merit Fellowship.

For more information, you can read my resume or LinkedIn profile.
