About Me

Hello! My name is Adam, and I am currently a PhD student studying in the Department of Statistics at the University of Michigan. My research advisor is Roderick Little, and my faculty mentor is Ben Hansen. I'm interested in figuring out methods for learning about and improving the social and political world we inhabit, and anything else that's cool.

In my spare time, I like to read about politics, history, and mythology, watch movies, play around with computers, and go hiking.

I recieved my B.A. in Quantitative Economics and Sociology from the University of California, Irvine in 2011. I am a member of Golden Key and Phi Beta Kappa, and a recipient of the Rackham Merit Fellowship.

For more information, check out my LinkedIn profile.
